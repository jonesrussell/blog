## LinkedIn

If your site serves a community, who you hand their browsing data to is a design decision, not an afterthought.

rhtcircle.ca now has first-party, self-hosted analytics: a collector, a dashboard, and page-stats, all running on our own infrastructure. No Google Analytics, no third-party tag soup, no shipping treaty members' reading habits to an ad network.

For a site about Indigenous governance and transparency, that alignment matters. You cannot ask people to trust a transparency project while quietly piping their visits to a surveillance-funded analytics product. The medium has to match the message.

The practical version is simple. You still get what you actually need: which pages get read, where people land, what is ignored. You just keep it. The data lives where the site lives, under the same control as everything else.

Self-hosting analytics used to be a chore. On a framework that already owns its storage and admin, it is just another module. Built on Waaseyaa.
