## LinkedIn

AI keeps speccing my projects on pre-AI timelines.

You sit down with Claude Code or whichever assistant to plan a new feature. You describe the surface, the slices, the constraints. The plan comes back: "5 work packages, 6 to 8 weeks." You shipped a 5-WP mission in a day yesterday.

This is the friction nobody talks about. Spec mode is where it shows up worst. The estimates are calibrated to a world that ended around 2023. A "2-week task" inside the plan is often something you can ship the same afternoon when the scope is honest. When you push back, the assistant cites "industry norms" and code review cycles and testing time. All real. None of which describe what actually happens when you and the AI co-author the work at full session-pace.

The cause is mechanical. Most public training data for "how long does this take" predates widespread AI assistance, so the model projects 2021 norms forward as if nothing changed.

The cost is drift, not just time. When you accept a long estimate to move on, the WP breakdown gets shaped by that estimate. Tasks get padded with imagined complexity. You implement a plan designed for a slower world and discover halfway through that half the WPs collapse into one.

The workaround is annoying because you have to repeat it. State the velocity assumption explicitly in your spec prompt. Reject the estimate during planning, not after. Cite recent evidence ("I shipped 5 WPs yesterday"). Strip duration estimates entirely when you can.

The honest framing is not "AI is bad at estimates." It is "AI is estimating on a baseline that the existence of AI itself made obsolete."
