## LinkedIn

Most people I talk to are confident they can tell AI writing from human writing.

I am not sure that holds up under a fair test.

So I put up a small web game called Spot the AI. You see two short passages side by side. One was written by a human author. One was written by Claude. You pick which one is the AI. That is the whole loop. No account, no setup, no scoring leaderboard.

The point is not to dunk on anyone. The point is calibration. If you cannot reliably tell the difference on short passages, that is useful information before you make claims about what AI writing sounds like, or before you build a hiring process, a classroom policy, or a content moderation rule around the assumption that you can.

It sits under OIATC, which is the broader push toward Indigenous-controlled AI tooling and infrastructure. Most of that work happens out of view. This one happens to be playable in a browser.

Play a few rounds and see how you do.

https://spot-the-ai.oiatc.ca
