## LinkedIn

A chatbot that makes things up is worse than no chatbot, especially on a treaty site. So this one is built the other way around.

rhtcircle.ca now has Anokii, an AI assistant seeded with the Robinson Huron Treaty corpus. Instead of a general model riffing from memory, it answers from the ingested content of the site itself: the land, the treaty, the resources, the records request. A member can ask a plain-language question and get an answer that points back at the actual pages.

The architecture is the interesting part. The site already models its content as a graph and already publishes a machine-readable layer. The assistant consumes that same structured corpus, so the thing the chatbot reads is the thing the site shows. There is no separate, stale knowledge base to drift out of sync.

This is what I want AI on a community site to be: not a generic helper bolted on the corner, but a way into the real material, grounded in sources the community controls. Anokii on Waaseyaa.
