## LinkedIn

A resource list is easy. A resource list that stays usable as it grows is the hard part.

rhtcircle.ca/resources just became a graph-driven, filterable card directory. Instead of a flat hand-maintained page, the resources are entities in the Waaseyaa graph, connected by topic and territory, and the page renders and filters them from that structure.

For a treaty member, that means you can come in looking for one thing, education funding, a records request, housing, and actually narrow to it, instead of scrolling a wall of links hoping the right one is there.

For whoever maintains it, the win is that the directory is data, not markup. Add a resource once, tag its topics, and it shows up everywhere it belongs: in the filtered view, in the related items, in the machine-readable feed. No copy to keep in sync across pages.

This is the kind of unglamorous infrastructure that decides whether a community site is still useful in a year, or a stale link dump. Built on Waaseyaa.
