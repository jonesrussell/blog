## LinkedIn

Software for a transparency movement should make the actual ask easy to join. So that is what we built.

rhtcircle.ca now hosts a records-request sign-on petition. A treaty member can read the request, understand what is being asked for and why, and add their name. The online signatures sit alongside the paper ones already gathered in person.

The feature itself is not complicated, a petition with a sign-on form. What makes it worth shipping is that it lowers the cost of participation to almost nothing. Someone who would never mail a form or make it to a meeting can still stand up and be counted in thirty seconds from their phone.

It was ported from an earlier campaign rather than rebuilt from scratch, which is one of the quiet benefits of building these community sites on a shared framework: a petition, an OG card system, an admin gate, you write it once and carry it to the next cause.

Built on Waaseyaa.
