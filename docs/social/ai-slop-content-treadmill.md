## LinkedIn

I built a machine that turns my git commits into social media posts.

A script mines my repos, scores what is postable, drafts it, then rewrites every idea three times: once for Bluesky, once for LinkedIn, once for Facebook. Four character budgets, four hashtag policies, four mental models of algorithms I cannot see.

I am a developer. I just want to share what I built and have a few of the right people notice. Instead I am a one-person content team optimizing for five recommendation engines.

Here is the uncomfortable part. My posts are not garbage. They are accurate, they link to real commits, they read fine. That is exactly the problem. "Reads fine" at infinite scale is what AI slop actually is. I am not flooding the feed with junk. I am flooding it with volume. Past a point those are the same thing.

The slop is not a content problem. It is an incentive problem. When the only way to be seen is to feed five algorithms daily, nobody can do that by hand and still ship code. So we automate, and automated competent voiceless content is the slop.

In the post I propose a few ways out: write once and syndicate from a canonical source, automate distribution but never authorship, cover fewer platforms on purpose, treat the reader as the tiebreaker when the algorithm wants something else, and disclose the assistance.

I do not have it solved. If I stop, I lose to the people who do not. If we all keep going, the feeds become unreadable. That is a coordination problem, and I would genuinely like to know how you handle it.
