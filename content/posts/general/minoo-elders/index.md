Minoo is an Indigenous platform for community-run programs. The newest module, [Minoo Elders](https://minoo.live/elders), is live, and this post is about what it does, why it exists, and what it needs from you to actually serve people.

## Why Elders Need More Than a Phone Tree

Look at any community running an Elders program and you'll find one person holding it together with a binder, a personal phone, and a patchy memory. Rides to medical appointments. Groceries when a winter storm hits. A burnt-out lightbulb. An hour of company on a long afternoon. The volunteers exist. The Elders exist. What's usually missing is the thing in the middle that connects them reliably.

That gap is the entire point of Minoo Elders.

## How Minoo Elders Works

Three people make the system go: an Elder (or a family member), a community coordinator, and a volunteer. The flow stays small on purpose.

1. An Elder submits a request: a ride, groceries, yard work, or a visit.
2. A community coordinator reviews the request and matches it to a nearby volunteer.
3. The volunteer reaches out, arranges the details, and shows up.
4. The coordinator follows up afterward to make sure it went well.

The follow-up is where most "match a helper to a person in need" software stops paying attention. Minoo runs the loop through step 4 because an Elder who had a bad ride usually won't call back to complain. They'll just stop calling. If you're the coordinator, your job is to catch that early.

For Elders who don't want to fill out a web form, there's a phone path. They call you directly and you enter the request for them. This isn't a fallback. For a lot of users, it's the primary path.

The form and the phone are the easy part of the story. The harder part is why Minoo built it this way at all.

## Why Indigenous-Led Design Matters Here

Elder care in Indigenous communities runs on different rails. Relationships matter more than tickets. Reputation matters more than process. If you're the coordinator, you already know half the Elders by name and you remember who takes their tea black.

Minoo is built around that reality instead of in spite of it. The coordinator is not a dispatcher in a call centre. They are a community member with authority and discretion, and the software exists to give them leverage, not to replace them.

That design choice generalizes well past Indigenous communities. Any neighbourhood with strong relationships and weak infrastructure can use the same model: rural towns, immigrant community organizations, faith communities, retirement co-ops. The pattern is the same. The coordinator is the system. The software is the leverage.

But leverage needs hands.

## Why Minoo Elders Needs Coordinators

Building the platform was the easy half. The hard half is finding the right coordinator in each community. Someone who picks up the phone. Someone who remembers to follow up. Someone who treats every Elder like family because, often, they are.

If that's you, or someone in your community, Minoo Elders is ready. The infrastructure is built. The form works. The phone path works. What it needs is people willing to run it.
